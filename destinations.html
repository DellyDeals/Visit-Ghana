Top Tourist Destinations in Ghana

Discover historic landmarks, wildlife, and natural wonders

Cape Coast Castle

Cape Coast Castle is one of Ghana’s most important historical landmarks. Built by European traders in the 17th century, the castle played a major role in the trans-Atlantic slave trade. Today, it serves as a museum and a place of reflection for visitors from around the world.

Historical Site

Kakum National Park

Kakum National Park is famous for its canopy walkway suspended high above the rainforest. The park is home to diverse plant and animal species and offers visitors a unique opportunity to experience Ghana’s rich rainforest ecosystem.

Nature & Adventure

Mole National Park

Mole National Park is Ghana’s largest wildlife reserve and a top destination for safari experiences. Visitors can observe elephants, antelopes, and other wildlife in their natural habitat while enjoying guided tours.

Wildlife Safari

Wli Waterfalls

Wli Waterfalls, located in the Volta Region, is the highest waterfall in West Africa. Surrounded by lush vegetation, it offers visitors a refreshing and scenic hiking experience.
